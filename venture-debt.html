COMMON INQUIRIES

Frequently Asked Questions

Direct, clear answers regarding venture debt structures and underwriting expectations.

What are equity warrants and how do they work?

Equity warrants are options granted to lenders to purchase a small percentage of company shares (typically 5% to 15% of the loan amount) at the valuation of the last or next equity round. Warrants align our interests with your long-term success but represent negligible dilution (typically 0.1% to 0.3% of the company's total equity) compared to standard VC rounds.

Do you require personal guarantees or collateral?

No. Capitar underwrites credit based on institutional equity backing, corporate enterprise value, and growth trajectories. We do not require personal guarantees from founders or hard asset collaterals (like real estate or machinery) that traditional commercial banks mandate.

What is the typical profile of a company you finance?

Our ideal partner company has raised at least one institutional round of equity (Seed or Series A), possesses 6+ months of cash runway, exhibits repeatable unit economics, and is scaling revenues. We finance cross-sector platforms, including SaaS, FinTech, Consumer Brands, EV/Mobility, and B2B Logistics.

Can we prepay the venture debt facility?

Yes. Capitar structures flexible prepayment clauses into our term sheets. If your company experiences rapid cash inflows or closes a massive equity round early, you can prepay the outstanding principal according to mutually structured prepayment parameters.
