Is your company a fit for venture debt?

We underwrite growth companies using specific parameters. Review our baseline criteria below.

1. Venture Backing

Company must be funded by institutional venture capital firms (Seed or Series A/B stages) with active board oversight.

2. Cash Runway

Minimum of 6 months of equity-backed cash runway remaining on the balance sheet at the time of credit evaluation.

3. Revenue Model

Demonstrable monthly revenues (typically ₹1 Cr+ monthly run rate) with repeatable unit economics and low cohort churn.

4. Clear Use Case

Capital must target scaling catalysts like working capital cycles, marketing ROI, battery/EV capex, or inventory cycles.

FOUNDER FAQ

Clarifications on underwriting & covenants

What is the difference in covenant packages between Capitar and commercial banks?

Traditional commercial banks require heavy financial covenants (such as strict debt-service coverage ratios and collateral backing) which early-growth startups cannot meet. Capitar structures minimal, flexible operating covenants (like minimum cash-balance requirements matching remaining runway) that align with normal venture cycles.

Can we raise debt if we plan to raise a Series B equity round in 6 months?

Yes. In fact, this is an ideal use case. Raising venture debt 6 months prior to a scheduled equity round allows you to extend your runway, continue hitting growth milestones, and negotiate your Series B valuation from a position of commercial strength, minimizing dilution.

How do you handle warrant dilution?

Warrants represent options to buy shares in the future, typically set at a fixed exercise price matching your last equity round. The warrant coverage is small (usually 5% to 15% of the total loan facility value). This translates to minor cap table dilution (usually less than 0.25%) and requires no cash outflow from the founders.
